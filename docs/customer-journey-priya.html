Friction
No tool surfaces architectural decay at the team scale.
Time-boxed review. No room for an exploratory tool that might waste 20 minutes.
Founder-y README. She has to dig for the engineer-quality signal.
No `--diff-only` mode. Full-repo audit is overkill for a PR review.
No confidence scoring → she can't filter for high-trust findings.
No way to flag a finding as "false positive" → no signal back to the system.
Verbose finding cards. She wants compact file:line + 1-line rationale, keyboard-driven.
No way to export findings as PR review comments automatically.
No CI integration. No GitHub Action. No PR-comment bot mode.
Opportunity
Team-tier positioning. "Catch the AI-generated regressions before they hit main."
20-minute PR mode. Hard time budget, prioritized findings only.
Eval transparency. Publish your precision/recall on a public benchmark. Engineers care.
`--diff-only` flag. Scope verification to changed files. The biggest single Priya unlock.
Confidence scoring baked into the Finding schema. Filter for ≥80% to skim, ≥95% to act.
False-positive marker on each finding. Captured in approvals.json. Telemetry feedback loop.
Power-user dense view. Keyboard nav, J/K to traverse, `f` to jump to file:line. Compact card mode.
Auto-post to PR. Reframe findings become inline review comments. Bot persona, configurable verbosity.
GitHub Action + PR-comment bot. CI-gate mode. Severity threshold. The merge-gate posture.
The trio · how they read Priya's map

Linnea Park

Principal Product Designer

Priya's UI needs are the opposite of Maya's: keyboard-driven, dense, fast. Same data, third lens. Build a "dense mode" toggle that compacts every finding card to one line with file:line, severity, and confidence. Power users want fewer pixels per finding, not more.

Mira Okonkwo

Brand & Design Director

Engineers don't want warmth, they want respect. The brand promise to Priya is "we won't waste your time." Every false positive damages that promise more than any feature improves it. The single most brand-positive thing you can do for her is publish your eval numbers and update them every release.

Rishi Madan

Staff ML Engineer

Priya is your eval-set author. She marks findings as false-positive in approvals.json — that's labeled data, free. The `--diff-only` mode + confidence scoring + FP-marker is a closed loop: she gives you the signal that makes the next run better for the next Priya. This is how the product gets sharper as it scales.
