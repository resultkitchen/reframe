Friction

Architecture-first messaging. "1 mapper + 6 agents" is your org chart, not her problem.
Config-first kills the honeymoon. 3 files to fill before any value.
Logs written for engineers. Silence reads as broken.
Wall of jargon. Only desktop screenshots. No priority. Overwhelm.

Opportunity
Hangover content series. Own the unspoken-dread SEO ("did Cursor break my app").
Audit-my-AI-app positioning. Specific high-intent landing pages per stack.
Lead with the review app, not the agents. Demo loop = the SPA, not the CLI.
Bootstrap mode. Infer brand from existing app, configure after first value.
Quiet progress strip ("Screen 14 of 34 — Patient intake"). Live finding tally.
Founder Digest first. Breakpoint strip (6 viewports). Dual-register language at source.
Resizable preview. Brand-voice + design agents. "Rewrite this in plain English" button per finding.
Plain-English diff explainer per file. Shareable run-summary card (the acquisition engine).
Learn approval patterns. Tasteful "Reviewed with Reframe" attribution = compounding word-of-mouth.
The trio · how they read this map

Linnea Park

Principal Product Designer · ex-Linear / ex-Stripe

Stages 4 and 6 are where you lose 70% of churn. Bootstrap mode solves 4. Founder Digest + dual-register language + breakpoint strip solve 6. Ship those before any new agent. The review app is the product — the CLI is the dev's surface.

Mira Okonkwo

Brand & Design Director · ex-W+K / ex-IDEO

Tone of every finding is the brand. "This page violates X" loses her. "When a logged-out user lands here, they hit a redirect loop — here's what they see" keeps her. Findings should read like a senior designer giving a junior a crit: warm, specific, opinionated, never blame-y. Voice is the product.

Rishi Madan

Staff ML Engineer · LLM evals & adapters

Two latent product surfaces hide in this map: the shareable artifact at Stage 8 and the personalization layer at Stage 9. Both ship from data you already collect in approvals.json and RunState. No new pipeline. Just new views.
